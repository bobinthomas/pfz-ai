## Design Context

### Users
Artisanal and small-boat fishermen in coastal Kerala. Low digital literacy; many read Tamil or Malayalam, not English. They use console-mounted Android tablets at arm's length in bright sunlight, often with wet hands and intermittent connectivity. The job: decide before leaving harbour whether to go out, where, and how confident the advice is.

### Brand Personality
**Honest · Coastal · Decisive.** Plain language over jargon. Confidence without false precision. Safety can interrupt. The interface should feel like a trusted harbour mate giving one clear answer — not a weather app or a data dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light, sunlight-legible theme (paper `#eef3f6`, navy `#0d2236`, teal `#0e7c86`). Maritime utilitarian with editorial warmth: harbour-chart geometry (bearing lines, distance rings, compass), tier colours as field signals (green/teal/amber/red). Display: Bricolage Grotesque; body: Hanken Grotesk; Indic: Noto Sans Tamil/Malayalam. Anti-references: dark-mode glow, cyan-purple gradients, generic card grids, weather-as-hero, Inter/Roboto defaults.

### Design Principles
1. One answer first — catch verdict dominates; everything else is progressive disclosure.
2. Boat-relative — "your best catch," not abstract zone rankings.
3. Confidence is honest — words + dot meter, never fake percentages in primary UI.
4. Sunlight-legible — large touch targets (≥48px), high contrast on light surfaces.
5. Offline is normal — staleness labelled calmly, never panic.
